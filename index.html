    • Research Interests

    I am interested in the high-dimensional entanglement and the orbital angular momentum of light in curved spacetime, including the following subjects.

  • High-dimensional orbital angular momentum entanglement in gravitational fluctuations

    • Recent years have witnessed significant progress in space communications with quantum entanglement, e.g., the quantum satellite Micius. As well known, twisted photons are competent candidates for future interstellar communications. However, the gravitational fluctuations are ubiquitous in spacetime. Thus, a fundamental question arises naturally as to how the gravitational fluctuations affect the coherence and the degree of high-dimensional orbital angular momentum entanglement. Here we answer this question positively by considering the covariant scalar Helmholtz equations and the Minkowski metric with fluctuations of Gaussian distribution. We succeeded in deriving analytically two-photon density matrices after traveling across the textures of curved spacetime. The degree of entanglement degradation is characterized by the purity and negativity, which reveals that higher-dimensional OAM entanglement is more susceptible to spacetime fluctuations. Our findings will be of fundamental importance for future interstellar quantum communications with twisted photons.

  • Orbital angular momentum entanglement in non-inertial reference frame

    • We examine a novel kind of entanglement degradation caused by the Unruh effect. We demonstrate that, from the perspective of an accelerating observer, an inertial empty space may seem to be full of particles, which may inundate and decay the high-dimensional entangled OAM photons. Additionally, we investigate entanglement in the vicinity of a Schwarzschild black hole. Our results suggest that higher-dimensional entanglement will suffer from greater adverse impacts in both instances.

  • Conversion between photons and gravitons with orbital angular momentum

    • It has been believed by many physicists that gravitational waves (GWs) can be quantized in the form of gravitons. Based on the similarities between electromagnetism and gravitation, a fundamental question arises naturally as to whether gravitons can carry quantized orbital angular momentum (OAM), can be entangled, or can reflect some aspects of the early Universe. We study the phenomenon of twisting and entangling gravitons in high-dimensional orbital angular momentum (OAM) states in the possible photon-graviton conversion. By exploring the quantum aspect of the conversion, we discover that the entanglements can oscillate between photons and gravitons, which holds promise for high-dimensional gravitational wave communications. In addition, we investigate the phenomenon that photons in the early Universe may be imprinted with OAM information from plasma vortices and turned into OAM gravitons by the presence of possible strong magnetic fields. The resultant OAM gravitational waves may exist in the current Universe and provide a novel way of studying the plasma vortex structure in the early Universe. Our findings will be of fundamental importance for understanding the nature of the early Universe as well as for future GW-based OAM quantum communications.
